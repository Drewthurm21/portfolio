Intro

~I have always been fascinated with building.~

I've built all manner of things from bicycles to GPU-mining rigs & bands to businesses. The single thread that runs through all of these experiences is the goal of creating something useful, inspiring and positive where there was once nothing. Software development offers a world of endless possibilities to do just that, and I'm excited to see what we can build together.

Work

~Learning to code has provided me the most interesting and engaging learning experiences of my adult life.~


Through the process of learning two separate tech stacks (JS/Express/Sequelize & Python/Flask/SQLAlchemy) I have been able to solidify my foundational knowledge of backend web architecture and RESTful APIs. Using both Pug and Jinja2 as templating engines as well as React & Redux has provided me an opportunity to work with some of the most popular technologies in the industry.

After cementing my understanding of core programming concepts in two different tech stacks with completely different syntaxes, I feel far more prepared to enter the workforce, acclimate and contribute to any team quickly.


~Recent Coding Projects~



For the first full-stack project at App Academy we would build a clone of goodreads.com functionality but employ our own style. We were given Friday and the weekend to plan, and the following Monday-Friday to code. We would be using JavaSript, Express / Sequelize and Pug.

Coding a full-stack website from scratch was an incredible learning experience! I the opportunity to conceptualize and implement a relational database, simple server architecture, RESTful API and backend routes. I also got to work with Pug as a templating engine to serve data driven HTML to the client. Please feel free to take a look at GoodGames here, and poke around as a demo user for added features.

Story

~The only way to do great work is to love what you do.~


My journey into programming began two years ago. I always thought learning to code would be an interesting and rewarding challenge so I purchased an introductory course covering Java to try it out. I enjoyed working my way through the course but when work got busy my hobbies hit the back burner.

A year later, during the downtime imposed by Kansas' Covid-19 shutdown, I dove in head first. I worked on App Academy's online program teaching Ruby and Ruby on Rails. I built simple apps like a console based hangman games & then quickly moved into OOP with a browser based tic-tac-toe game. They were simple apps, but they were interactive and fun. It was all very exciting and I quickly fell in love.

Business management is a fun and challenging profession, and some of the lessons I learned in my leadership roles have been invaluable both in business and my personal life. But with ten years of experience in the rushes, derailments, trials and triumphs of the GM world I realized my passion for it was gone. I wasn't excited about it anymore. I realized it was time to make a change.

~I began putting the pieces together to facilitate a career change.~

Following talks with my boss I was tasked with training my replacement throughout the month of August, 2020. September first I would begin applying to bootcamps and within 1 month I would be accepted at HackReactor, FlatIron School and App Academy. I was left with a tough but promising decision to make.

After careful consideration of the tech stacks these schools offered, I decided to attend App Academy. A rigorous 1000-hour bootcamp with a solid industry reputation and a ~3% acceptance rate. The course teaches full-stack web development. They employ a heavy focus on JavaScript and Python with Flask and Flask Alchemy, for backend frameworks & teach React / Redux / CSS / SCSS as well as Pug and Jinja2 for front end development and templating. I am set to graduate on June 4th 2021.
