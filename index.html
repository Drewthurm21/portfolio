Story

~The only way to do great work is to love what you do.~


My journey into programming began two years ago. I always thought learning to code would be an interesting and rewarding challenge so I purchased an introductory course covering Java to try it out. I enjoyed working my way through the course but when work got busy my hobbies hit the back burner.

A year later, during the downtime imposed by the Covid-19 shutdown, I dove in head first. I worked on App Academy's online program teaching Ruby and Ruby on Rails. I built simple apps like a console based hangman games & then quickly moved into OOP with a browser based tic-tac-toe game. They were simple apps, but they were interactive and fun. It was all very exciting and I quickly fell in love.

Business management is a fun and challenging profession, and some of the lessons I learned in my leadership roles have been invaluable both in business and my personal life. But with ten years of experience in the rushes, derailments, trials and triumphs of the GM world I realized my passion for it was gone. I wasn't excited about it anymore. I realized it was time to move on.

~I began putting the pieces together to facilitate a career change.~

Following talks with my boss I was tasked with training my replacement throughout the month of August, 2020. September first I would begin applying to bootcamps and within 1 month I would be accepted at HackReactor, FlatIron School and App Academy. I was left with a tough but promising decision to make.

After careful consideration of the tech stacks these schools offered, I decided to attend App Academy. A rigorous 1000-hour bootcamp with a ~3% acceptance rate and a solid industry reputation. The course teaches full-stack web development. They employ a heavy focus on JavaScript/Express/Seqelize || Python/Flask/Flask Alchemy for backend & HTML/CSS/React/Redux/ as well as Pug and Jinja2 for front end development and templating. I graduated June 4th 2021.

~Time to embark on a new journey.~
