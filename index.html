Intro

~I have always been fascinated with building.~


Throughout my life I've always been interested in how things work. I've always been a tinkerer. I've built all manner of things from bycicles to GPU mining-rigs... bands to businesses and the single thread that runs through all of these projects is that I am consistently inspired by creating something useful and positive out of nothing. Software development offers a world of endless possibilities with which to do so & I can't believe it has taken me this long to start.


~I have spent the majority of my working life as a business manager but I have recently moved into the software engineering space.~


My journey into coding began three years ago while searching for a new hobby. I purchased an introductory course covering Java and enjoyed working my way through it but when work got busy my hobbies hit the back burner and, for now at least, a simple calculator was my greatest creation. During the downtime imposed by Kansas' Covid-19 shutdown I decided to explore coding again. I worked on App Academy's online program teaching Ruby and Ruby on Rails. I built simple things like a console based hangman game then quickly moved into OOP with a browser based tic-tac-toe game. Simple apps, but they were interactive and fun. It was exciting and I quickly fell in love.

Management is a fun and challenging environment, and some of the lessons I learned in my leadership roles have been invaluable both in business and my personal life. But with ten years of experience in the rushes, derailments, trials and triumphs of the GM world I realized my passion for it was gone. I wasn't excited about it anymore. I realized it was time to make a change.

~I began putting the pieces together to facilitate a career change.~

After talks with my boss I would be tasked with training my replacement throughout the month of August, 2020. My last day would be the 28th and I would depart with the goal of being enrolled in the upcoming spring semester. September first I began applying to bootcamps.

I would apply to two local bootcamps, as well as the top 3 ranked schools I could find. After the initial aptitude test results had returned I was offered behavioral interviews at HackReactor, FlatIron School and App Academy. I was left with a tough but promising decision to make.

After careful consideration of the tech stacks these schools offered, I decided to attend App Academy App Academy. A rigorous 1000 hour bootcamp with a solid industry reputation and a ~3% acceptance rate. The course teaches full-stack web development. They employ a heavy focus on JavaScript using Express and Sequelize, Python with Flask and Flask Alchemy, for backend frameworks & teach React / Redux / CSS / SCSS as well as Pug and Jinja2 for front end development and templating. After passing the behavioral interview, and multiple challenge modules I earned a seat in their December, 2020 cohort.

~I graduate on June 7th 2021.~
